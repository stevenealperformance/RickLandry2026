Zone-by-zone read-out

Why slope was misleading. A raw SmO2 slope of −0.03 %/s looks alarming, but if that's happening at 250 W instead of 180 W, it's a totally different story. The athlete is producing 40 % more power — a faster SmO2 drop is expected. Normalizing by power (SmO2 mean ÷ watts) answers the real question: at this power output, how much oxygen is available in the muscle? Higher ratio = better economy.

SmO2/W rises at every intensity, every block. The cleanest story in this dataset. Ten of twelve protocol slots show SmO2/W climbing Mar 25 → Apr 17 (the exceptions — B2 80 %, B2 85 %, B3 75 % — all have Mar 25's numbers artificially inflated because that session's actual HR didn't reach the protocol targets, so the "power at that window" is wrong). Where the comparison is clean, the trend is monotonic.

B1 85 % is the most dramatic. SmO2/W went 0.054 → 0.099 → 0.123 → 0.126 — more than 2.3× better economy at the protocol's hardest zone. Mar 25 produced 257 W at 13.9 % SmO2. Apr 17 produced 252 W at 31.8 % SmO2. Same power, far more oxygen in the tissue, same heart rate. That's textbook aerobic adaptation.

P:HR agrees. The cardiovascular side of the same story — watts per beat is up most strongly in Block 2 (+0.33 at B2 85 %) and Block 3 (+0.27 at B3 75 %). Block 1 doesn't show much P:HR movement because the athlete was already near ceiling when fresh. But Block 1's SmO2/W did move a lot — meaning the adaptation isn't about cardiac output, it's about muscle-level oxygen utilization.

The combined picture. Rising P:HR (more power per beat, cardiovascular) PLUS rising SmO2/W (more oxygen at the muscle per watt, peripheral) in the same sessions is the strongest possible reading. The adaptation is happening at both levels. The slope data was hiding this story because it wasn't normalized.

Working read

The ratios confirm what the raw slopes couldn't — this athlete is producing more power with more oxygen headroom at every matched intensity. The fatmax ceiling has clearly shifted upward. A continuous 60–90 min effort at 195–210 W watching SmO2 drift would confirm the new sustainable band. Based on Apr 17's B3 80 % (226 W at 43.6 % SmO2 mean), 210 W should be well within aerobic territory.
