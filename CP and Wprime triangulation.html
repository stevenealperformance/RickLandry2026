At 2–5 minutes, Stride and Xert curves predict power 50–80 W above her actual 90-day MMP, because their larger W' values inflate the short-duration prediction. The 2-param curve for Stride at 2 min is 535 W; her actual 2-min MMP is 373 W. Not a problem with Stride's data, a problem with reading a 2-param curve literally at durations under 3 minutes.

At 20–60 minutes, the five curves converge because this is the CP-dominated region. The recommended curve (295/14) sits right on top of the race NP peaks at 20-min (+4 W), 30-min (+5 W), 40-min (+1 W), and 60-min (+17 W). That's the best joint match of any of the five models in this window.

03

Why the W' values range from 11.8 to 30.1 — models, not physiology

The CP estimates agree within ~30 W. The W' estimates span a 2.5× range. Both things can be true because different platforms use different models, and W' means something slightly different in each.

Model familyW' definitionTypical value for Mona
Monod / Skiba (2-param)Integral of power above CP until task failure at very short duration (≤3 min)11–15 kJ
Morton 3-paramSame as above but with a P_max asymptote — soaks up more of the 30s–1min region into W'15–20 kJ
Xert MPADynamic Maximum Power Available model — larger reservoir with separate recovery kinetics16–22 kJ
Stride (hyperbolic variant)Extended-range model that includes the full Pmax-to-CP gap in the anaerobic pool25–32 kJ
Operational implication: The W' number must match the model your tooling uses. If intervals.icu / TrainingPeaks run a Skiba W'bal, use a Skiba-compatible W' (13–15 kJ). Do not import Stride's 30 kJ or Xert's 18.7 kJ into a Skiba calculation — the math will be wrong. If you want to switch tooling to Xert or Stride for W'bal tracking, use their native W' inside that platform only.

The triangulation for CP is valid across models because CP is defined consistently across the classical, Morton, and Xert/Stride formulations — it's the power at which the anaerobic reservoir asymptotically empties. So when Stride says 285 and Xert says 299, those numbers are directly comparable to my 290 conservative fit. They're telling the same story.

05

Final recommendation

Set CP = 290 W / W' = 14.5 kJ immediately for training prescription and W'bal tracking in intervals.icu. This sits at the center of the Skiba-family cluster (Stride 285 · intervals.icu @ 180s 289 · my hybrid 290), uses a Skiba-compatible W' at the midpoint of the 13–15.7 kJ range from the same cluster, and aligns with what your own primary tool says when queried with the shorter-duration window.

Why 290 rather than 295 or 299: The intervals.icu @ 180s data point (289) is the strongest single anchor because it's what your tool produces natively when asked to be more short-duration-weighted. Stride is at 285. My hybrid fit is at 290. Those three numbers average 288, and 290 is a round landing point. The 295 number from the v2 version was a compromise between 285 and 305 (my aggressive); with intervals@180s at 289 pulling the cluster tighter, 290 is now more defensible than 295.

Why W' = 14.5 kJ rather than 13, 15.7, 18.7, or 30: 14.5 kJ is the midpoint of the two Skiba-compatible estimates (my 13 and intervals.icu's 15.7). That's the right number to plug into your platform's W'bal calculation. Xert's 18.7 kJ and Stride's 30 kJ live inside their own platforms and should stay there.

The one thing working against this recommendation: Xert's 299 / 18.7 is the model that produces the cleanest race diagnostics (IF 1.04, only 7.6% W'bal pinning). If Xert turns out to be closer to truth than the Skiba-family cluster, we'd under-prescribe by ~9 W. That's why the 20-min test matters — it resolves the Skiba-vs-Xert tension.

Confirmation test, next 7–10 days:

  • 15-min warm-up, 3 × 1-min openers at 120% FTP, 5-min easy
  • 20-min all-out effort on a sustained climb or flat road
  • Target: 305–313 W average for the 20 min. That confirms CP ≈ 290.
  • If she holds 315–322 W → CP is closer to 299 (Xert is right); revise upward.
  • If she holds 325+ W → CP is at my aggressive 305 or higher; revise upward again.
  • If she holds < 305 W → investigate fatigue, illness, power meter calibration before revising downward.
